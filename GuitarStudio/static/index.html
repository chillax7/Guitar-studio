Dry takes for this song

Only "dry" recordings (guitar only, no backing track baked in) are listed here — a regular Play Along take can't be scored accurately, since it already contains the reference performance mixed in with yours.

Record a dry take

Captures only your guitar rig's output (post-amp/pedals) — never the backing track — so scoring compares your playing against the reference on its own, not a mix that already contains it.

This is different from the takes recorded in Play Along's Record tab, which deliberately mix the backing track in with your guitar so you have a normal, watchable/listenable performance video afterward. That's the right choice for a take you want to watch back — but the wrong input for scoring, since the reference performance would already be baked into the file regardless of how well you actually played. Use Play Along's Record tab for a real performance recording; use this button when you specifically want a take to score here.

Welcome to Orpheus Guitar Studio

  1. Import a song — drag an audio file onto the sidebar, or click it.
  2. Separate — pick a model (top right) and click Separate. Takes a minute or two; splits the song into stems (drums, bass, vocals, guitar, …).
  3. Mix — mute/solo/fade each stem, paint a mute region over a section you want gone (e.g. the original guitar), loop a section (drag the ruler handles), drop + Markers on a solo and double-click one to loop it, Speed Trainer to practice it slow then step up to speed.
  4. Play Along (🎸 in the sidebar) — plug in your guitar, pick an amp (Clean / Analog / a real captured amp via Neural), shape the tone, add cab/EQ/comp/delay/reverb in whatever order you like (drag the ⠿ handles), and record your take (camera optional).
  5. Export (right-hand panel) once the mix sounds right.

Every heuristic in this app (key/tempo detection, tone matching, riff-loudness auto-calibration, …) is a starting point to confirm by ear, never a guaranteed answer — see USER-MANUAL.md in the app folder for the full picture, including the honesty notes on exactly where each one's limits are.
